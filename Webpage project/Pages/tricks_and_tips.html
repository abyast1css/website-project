Master Skateboarding Tricks

Learn essential techniques and tricks ranging from beginner to advanced. Learn from experience skaters how to do more complex tricks.

Beginner

Push & Balance

How to do it:

  1. Stand on the skateboard with your back foot on the tail and front foot near the middle
  2. Keep your knees slightly bent for better balance
  3. Push off the ground with your back foot in a smooth, controlled motion
  4. Place your pushing foot back on the board between your two feet
  5. Distribute your weight evenly across the board
  6. Keep your arms out for balance and your eyes forward

Turning & Carving

How to do it:

  1. Start by rolling forward at a comfortable speed
  2. Shift your weight to your front foot to turn in that direction
  3. Lean your body into the turn while bending your knees
  4. Apply slight pressure with your toes (heel side) or heels (toe side) to engage the board
  5. Use your shoulders to guide the turn smoothly
  6. Practice wide arcs before attempting tight turns

Stopping Safely

How to do it:

  1. Foot Braking: Drag your back foot on the ground to slow down gradually
  2. Tail Drag: Lean back to lift the nose and drag the tail on the ground
  3. Start with foot braking as it's the easiest method
  4. Always slow down before stopping completely
  5. Avoid sudden stops which can throw you off balance
  6. Practice these techniques in empty, flat areas first

Falling Properly

How to do it:

  1. Try to fall forward rather than backward when possible
  2. Roll with the fall to distribute impact across your body
  3. Protect your wrists by tucking and rolling on your forearms
  4. Avoid putting your hands flat out to catch yourself
  5. Tuck your chin to protect your head
  6. Always wear protective gear: helmet, wrist guards, elbow and knee pads

Intermediate Tricks

Ollie

Master the fundamental ollie - the foundation for most skateboard tricks. This involves popping the tail while sliding your front foot to lift the board into the air smoothly.

Kickflip

Execute a smooth kickflip by flicking the edge and catching your board mid-air. Timing and foot placement are crucial for consistent kickflips every time.

Manual

Balance on your rear wheels and ride with style and control. Manuals require excellent balance and precise body positioning to maintain the trick consistently.

Nose Manual

The opposite of a manual, balancing on your front wheels with skill. This trick is great for building overall board control and balance expertise.

Advanced Tricks

Heelflip

Execute a heelflip - the opposite of a kickflip with increased difficulty. Flick the heel edge and catch it with the same technique as a kickflip but reversed.

Shove-It

Rotate your board without flicking - a foundation for combo tricks. This movement forms the basis for many advanced trick variations you'll learn.

Varial Kickflip

Combine a shove-it and kickflip in one fluid motion for the ultimate trick. This requires precise timing and coordination between flicking and board rotation.

Tre flip

A combination of a 360 shove-it and a kickflip for maximum style. This advanced trick requires excellent pop and flick control for proper execution.
